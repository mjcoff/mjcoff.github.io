# Milo Coffey

I am a PhD student in Linguistics at Swansea University. My research focuses on how lay people and healthcare professionals understand and interpret synonymous medical words, like *fractured* bone and *broken* bone.

I graduated with a BA in English Language from Swansea University in 2021. After a year spent working first as a research assistant and then as a tutor, I was awarded an ESRC WGSSS studentship to study for a MSc in Social Research Methods (graduated 2023) and subsequently a PhD at Swansea University.

My research interests lie mainly in lexical semantics, and ways of applying theoretical approaches to word meaning to real-life contexts. I have presented my work at conferences and, together with Professor Tess Fitzpatrick and colleagues at Swansea Bay University Health Board, I have published a journal article about words and meanings in cancer communication. I am a Senior Teaching Assistant at the School of Culture and Communication, Swansea University, where I have delivered sessions to undergraduate linguistics students on a variety of topics.
